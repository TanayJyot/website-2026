# conditional probability
##
- we are only considering outcomes from A and B that are a part of B because B is a **given** in our case.
> The proportion of P(B) that A also occurs.

$P(A | B)$
- Here $A \subseteq B$ 
- The [[probability function]] still applies.
- $P(A^\complement |B) = 1 - P(A)$ 

### Multiplication Rule 
- $P(A \cap B) = P(A | B) \times P(B)$ 
- Similarly $P(A \cap B) = P(B|A)  \times P(B)$
- Since $P(A|B)=\frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$ 
- [[chain rule for probability]]
- [[law of total probability]]
- $P(A \text{ and } B) = P(B|A)P(A)$
