# excitatory signal
##
- When a neuron receives an *excited* signal from another neuron, the probability of the [[neuron]] firing increases. 
- They affect the cell by making it less negative/more positive than the [[resting potential]]

- The interaction between neurotransmitters and their receptors can have one of two effects on the receiving neuron: excitation or inhibition. When a neurotransmitter has an excitatory effect, it slightly depolarizes the receiving neuron, increasing the likelihood that the neuron will reach threshold and initiate an action potential. Recall that depolarization reduces the difference between the electrical environments inside and outside the neuron. [[inhibitory signal]]
