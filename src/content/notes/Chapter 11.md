## 11.3 
### 1. Explain what the the phrase “internalize the externality” means for a polluting business. Give an example of how government policies like emissions taxes “internalize externalities” for polluters to create smart social choices.
For a polluting business, [[internalize the externality]] would mean that the business would have to pay money for the pollution they do making it a [[Marginal private cost]] for the business. This is done by [[Emissions Tax]] that is the tax on the company based on the pollution they do. Thus because of the tax, [[marginal social cost]] = [[Marginal private cost]], thus creating smart  social choices.

> [!question] 2. Some environmental groups try to expose businesses that pollute while supporting environmentally friendly businesses by posting information and photos on public websites. Explain how this strategy may “internalize the externality” for the polluters even without government action.
- Businesses would be forced to reduce their pollution to save their reputation so since pollution would decrease as would external cost thus when external cost = 0. [[Marginal private cost]] = [[marginal social cost]] thus our job is done.
