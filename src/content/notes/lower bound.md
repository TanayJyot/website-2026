# Omega“Lower Bound”
## [[CSC110/Lectures/Week 8 Lecture 2]]
- An $\Omega$ that you take such that the function you want can be restricted lower 
## [[9.3]]
- This is like the opposite of [[Big ‘O’ Notation]]. Big O looks for the upper bound while Omega looks for lower bound.
- Here, $g(n) \ge c \cdot f(n)$ 


## 
- Lower bound on algorithms as a whole
- For any problem $C(P)$ - the best worse case running time that solves P.
	- The upper bound is specific to the algorithm itself
	- All algorithms solving a particular problem require a certain amount of time which acts as their lower bound. 
- So we prove lower bounds on classes of algorithms.
