## Union:
$$ A \cup B = \{x : x \in A \lor x \in B \} $$ ^006892

If the element belongs to either A or B then it belongs to A union B. 

- This is not the same as addition in something like [[logic gates 1]]. Because of the [[cardinality]]. The cardinality here will not increase directly here because some elements may be same between A and B and will only be counted once. 

### Infinite sets of unions: 
There can be infinite sets of unions if they match certain conditions. 
#### Example: 
##### 1. 
For each $$ t \ge 0 $$
Define: $$ A_t = \{x \in \Re : (\exists t \ge 0) 0 \le x \le t\}$$

Every smaller union will be a subset of the acceding larger union because the larger union will contain the smaller union.
