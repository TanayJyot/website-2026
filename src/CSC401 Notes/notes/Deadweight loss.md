# Deadweight loss
- Lower [[Total surplus]] than an [[Efficient Market Outcome]]. 

![[Pasted image 20231008231706.png]]
![[Pasted image 20231008231801.png]]
- This is because all of this excess supply and excess demand is useless because no one wants it at that price and that waste is what is making it inefficient. 
- It is about what you could have had if the quantity wasn’t restricted the way it is.
- Quantity will be lost because [[trade]] cannot happen around that quantity
- If the surplus transferred between consumer surplus and producer surplus = deadweight loss, then 1 dollar is lost for every dollar transferred.
- The more [[Elasticity|elastic]] the market the more the deadweight loss.
