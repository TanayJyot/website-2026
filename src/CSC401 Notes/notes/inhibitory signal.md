# inhibitory signal
##
- opposite of [[excitatory signal]]
- [^1]

[^1]: What would even happen if the inhibitory signal occurs on a neuron at [[resting potential]]? How does it matter if it becomes more negative? When and how does it do that? #psy100/question 

When a neurotransmitter has an inhibitory effect, it slightly hyperpolarizes the receiving neuron, moving the cell farther from threshold and reducing the likelihood that it will initiate an action potential. Recall that hyperpolarization increases the difference between the electrical environments inside and outside the neuron.
